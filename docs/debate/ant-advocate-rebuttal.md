## 1. Natural-language rebuttal

The Spider Sub-Agent argued well, and on the framing I largely agree: the
curve must close because the _enemy got more dangerous_, not because the
player out-leveled a statue. That is the "boring-but-balanced failure mode"
(§6.2) and it is as bad for the ant fantasy as it is for the spider one — a
glass cannon with nothing to be a cannon _against_ is just glass. So I open
by conceding the spider's through-line is correct, and then fight only where
the _placement_ of the spider's escalation actually craters the ant
experience or breaks the §5 floor. Honest convergence where the spider is
right is what makes my held positions worth the arbiter's weight.

### 1.1 Spider claim #2 — heal-priority web-defense ON at L1: **CONCEDED**

I implied in my opening (Placement F, and the L1 section) that spider power
should be deferred and that L1 should foreground _ant_ primitives. The
spider's counter is correct and I concede it cleanly. The L1 win condition
is literally "capture the spider-web (defended POST template)" (§4.1). A web
that does not heal-prioritize is not a web — it is a generic POST, and the
tutorial fails to teach the single most important enemy fact: _spiders
defend, and you do not dribble parties at a defended target_. That lesson is
**pro-ant pedagogy**, not anti-ant: it is exactly the "mass and commit"
discipline that the glass-cannon doctrine lives or dies on. An ant player
who learns at L1 to concentrate force and time the burst is an ant player
who survives L6 eradication. So heal-priority at L1 actively _serves_ the
ant doctrine by teaching it.

My one binding condition, which the spider already offered ("tune the heal
magnitude down, don't remove the mechanic"): the L1 heal magnitude must be
low enough that a _correctly massed_ ant assault — the lesson the level
teaches — wins decisively, not marginally. Legible hardness means the
player can see that their _mistake_ (dribbling) lost, not that the mechanic
is opaque. Mechanism conceded; I hold only that magnitude tuning is a
named L1 exit gate, not a free spider knob. This is convergence: I withdraw
the implied "defer spider power" stance at L1.

### 1.2 Spider claim #1 — POST-occupation bonus by L6, "non-negotiable": **PARTIALLY CONCEDED**

This is the spider's strongest argument and I will not pretend otherwise.
The logic is airtight on its own terms: L6 is eradication (§4.3.2,
"turtling does nothing… timeout likely ant-loss"). A turtling spider AI in
an eradication map is _trivially farmed_ — the player just walks up the
stairs and picks off a static cluster. That is not a 55% scenario; it is
the ant-stomp the spider names, and it would betray the very doctrine I
defend (L6 is supposed to be the honest pre-finale low point — I _endorsed_
~55% in my opening). A proactive spider that comes down to contest
stair-landings is what makes L6 a _game_. I concede the mechanism must be
live by L6.

Where I do **not** fully concede, and where I hand the arbiter a concrete
alternative: "by L6" must not silently become "at L4 or L5." POST-occupation
bonus is a _proactivity_ lever, not a _combat-power_ lever — but its
interaction with the L4 plane-asymmetry cluster I argued against is
multiplicative, not additive. A spider that both flanks freely across planes
_and_ is incentivized to rush POSTs is not a 60% L4; it is a curve-breaker.
So my concrete counter-proposal:

- **POST-occupation bonus debuts at L6, the eradication scenario itself** —
  the scenario whose entire design _requires_ it. This is the latest the
  spider can credibly demand and the earliest I can credibly grant without
  the L4 stack compounding. It is not a deferral past the spider's stated
  floor; it lands exactly _at_ it.
- If the within-scenario loop shows L5 going to a turtle-stomp without it,
  the fallback is a **proactivity-only variant at L5** (the AI _values_
  occupied POSTs for target-selection but gains _no combat bonus_ from
  them) — behavior change without a balance lever. This gives the spider
  the "spiders come out and play" texture a scenario earlier without
  handing them the curve-bending power knob.

That is genuine convergence: I accept the non-negotiable on mechanism and
floor; I contest only the _combat-bonus magnitude leaking earlier than L6_,
and I give the arbiter a falsifiable test (does L5 turtle-stomp in-loop?)
rather than an assertion.

### 1.3 Spider claim #3 — hypnotize weak L4/L5 → full power L8: **PARTIALLY CONCEDED, with a tightened debut**

The spider framed this as an honest concession and it largely is. My
opening's concern was "mechanics that hurt ants early should be deferred,"
and the spider's L8-only _full power_ with a _weak_ L4/L5 debut substantially
satisfies it. A first-contact full-strength hypnotize deciding the L8
recruit-or-die objective (§4.3.3) is the textbook anti-fun pattern I named
for _recruit_ in my opening — the spider correctly applies the same logic to
_its own_ tool. I credit that; it is the kind of cross-faction reasoning the
debate format is supposed to produce.

I hold on two specifics:

1. **Debut at L5, not L4.** L4 is already the §5 curve's hardest dip (~60%),
   the POST-randomization shock, and the natural home for the
   plane-asymmetry cluster + combos (my Placements B/C). Adding _even a weak_
   hypnotize debut to L4 violates §3.4.3 in spirit — the player cannot name
   "what's new this level" in one sentence when L4 already carries
   randomization + asymmetry + combos. L5 (bedroom, concealment POSTs,
   §4.1) is the spider's own proposed home and is _better_ on its merits: a
   hypnotized neutral emerging from a concealment POST is thematically
   coherent and L5 is a rebound scenario (~65%) with cognitive headroom.
   So: **hypnotize light debut at L5, full power L8.** This is closer to
   the spider's fallback than its lead ask, and the spider explicitly
   offered L5 as "a natural home."
2. **"Weak" must be specified as control-_duration_ capped, not just
   neutral-density capped.** A short-duration hypnotize the ant can play
   around teaches the _concept_ (your units / neutrals can be turned)
   without the feel-bad of losing a committed party for a decisive number
   of turns. Low neutral density alone still allows a single catastrophic
   long flip. Duration cap is the load-bearing knob for learner-safety;
   I want it named.

Net: concede the L4/L5-debut→L8-climax structure; hold the debut to L5
specifically and pin "weak" to a duration cap.

### 1.4 NEW contested point the spider opening exposed — venom-blast at L3

My opening argued venom-blast + spider blitz gated to **L6+**. The spider
counter-proposed **venom-blast at L3** as "the honest middle" — a
single-target, low-cognitive-load damage tool that gives the spider AI a
reason to initiate. This is the sharpest _new_ disagreement and I do not
concede it, but I move toward it: I will trade L3 for a **bounded L4**.

Reasoning from ant experience: my opening's objection to early venom-blast
was specifically _sustained_ damage punishing the low-HP ant in _protracted_
fights before the player has the burst toolkit (combos L4, cards L7,
promotion L3). The spider is right that a _single-target, single-instance_
venom-blast is not the attrition tool I was guarding against — that is a
_burst_ answer, and an ant player who has _bio-evolution + charisma
promotion at L3_ (locked, §3.4.5; my Placement D) actually has a contemporary
answer to it. The remaining problem is purely the §5 floor: L3 targets 70%.
A new spider _offensive initiation_ tool at the _first_ scenario the spider
can initiate at all is more than the "single small swing" the spider claims
when stacked on pheromone-erasure (L2, cumulative) with no ant burst combo
yet (combos are L4). My concrete alternative:

- **Venom-blast debuts L4, not L3.** L4 is the licensed ~60%
  spike-toward-spider; a spider damage-initiation tool is an _honest engine_
  of that spike (the spider made exactly this argument for plane-switch at
  L4 — it applies identically here). At L4 the ant _also_ gets combos
  (my Placement C, unchanged): Royal Onslaught is the burst counter that
  makes "spiders can now spit venom" read as escalation-with-an-answer
  rather than unanswered chip damage at 70%.
- This keeps the spider's "not L7+, spiders aren't punching bags for five
  scenarios" concern fully satisfied — L4 is mid-tier, four scenarios
  before the spider's own Venom Storm combo at L7, exactly the runway the
  spider wanted (L4 component → L7 combo is a clean 3-scenario runway, only
  one shorter than the spider's L3→L7 ask, and the spider's own §1.5 said
  the runway requirement is "long enough that the player understands the
  component" — three scenarios clears that).

So: I reject L3, I do not push to L6+ (my opening's position — I move off
it, because the spider correctly distinguished burst from attrition), I
land on **L4 paired with the ant's combo answer**. That is convergence,
not intransigence.

### 1.5 Where the spider and I already agree (log for the arbiter)

- **Retreat + threat-assessment at L2.** The spider wants it for ambush
  identity; my opening wanted retreat at _L1_ (both sides) for ant
  learner-safety. These are not in conflict — retreat-the-posture at L1
  (the low-HP unit must be able to pull out in the tutorial) and
  retreat-as-spider-_threat-assessment-behavior_ maturing at L2 are
  compatible under cumulative addition. I hold L1 for the _posture_ (ant
  learner-safety is non-negotiable for a glass-cannon tutorial) and concede
  the _spider behavioral layer_ (threat-assess, decline-and-re-engage)
  arrives L2. No contest; flagging the distinction so the arbiter does not
  read a conflict that is not there.
- **Day/night at L10, blitz debut ~L7.** Endorsed in my opening, endorsed
  by the spider. Closed.
- **No hypnotize/venom/asymmetry/erasure at L1.** Mutual. Closed.

### 1.6 The one place I still hold hard — asymmetric plane-switch + plane-affinity not before L4

The spider argued plane-switch at L4 on §5-curve grounds ("L4 is the
licensed spike, denying it flattens the curve"). I _agree it lands at L4_ —
that was my opening position (Placement B: "no earlier than L4"). We
converge on L4. The residual disagreement is narrow and I state it
precisely for the arbiter: the spider's structured summary 2.4 concedes a
"range-limited corner-passive if pushed." I push. At L4, plane-switch +
plane-affinity + POST-randomization + (my) combos + (now) venom-blast is
_five_ deltas. Cumulative addition means L4 is the heaviest scenario in the
tier. The spider's own §3.4.3 discipline (which the spider invoked against
its _own_ hypnotize-at-L2 over-reach) applies here: **plane-switch ships
range-limited at L4** (fewer corner-pairs active), reaching full corner
coverage at **L5 or L6**. This is the spider's own offered concession; I am
asking the arbiter to bank it as a _condition of L4 placement_, not a
later courtesy.
