## 1. Natural-language argument — opening

### L5 is the spider's banked payoff scenario — the plane-affinity completion is the spec working

My round-1 through-line stands: the curve must close because the
_enemy got more dangerous_, not because the player out-leveled a statue
(§6.2, boring-but-balanced). L5 is the scenario where the spider finally
collects what it was _promised three arbitrations ago_. The L3
arbitration §4 explicitly banked the spider combat `wall` armor
sub-field (`+1/0 → +1/+1`) and full corner coverage to L5, with the
arbiter's own forward-consistency words: "at L5 (Bedroom, all 6 planes
reopened) the spider combat `wall` row deepens `+1/0 → +1/+1` (the
banked armor sub-field) and the gradient reaches full corner coverage …
the gap closes by the spider catching up off-floor, which is the
curve's intended closure mechanism." The L4 arbitration §3.3 reinforced
the doctrine: the spider's _favored-side_ payoff is _sequenced_ — L4 was
the licensed dip (favored the defender by being a dip), the spider's
deeper edges land at their designed homes. L5 is one of those homes:
the first scenario reopened to all 6 planes (level-progression-plan §2
L5), where off-floor affinity finally has the plane space to _mean
something_.

I am not relitigating placement — every L5 component is ruled.
Plane-affinity full corner coverage at L5 is ruled §3.B and banked by
the L3 arbitration §4. Hypnotize light debut at L5 is ruled §3.H.
Recruit-as-order at L5 is ruled §3.C. Under-Bed concealment is the
Level PA's L5 placement, fog-immunity rule Gameplay-owned (§4a
override). All conceded. My job here is the same as at L3 and L4:
defend the _substance_ of the spider's banked payoff so "the curve
rebounds" does not get tuned into "the spider's promised step
evaporates so the ant tool can dominate uncontested."

### The plane-affinity completion must register — it is the banked callback, not a footnote

The L3 arbitration §3 gave the ant the residual integer (`spider wall
+1/0`, attack-only) _explicitly on the promise_ that the armor
sub-field was "the L5 payoff" — "the spider is not denied its identity,
it is given the ramp it argued for its own tools." That promise is a
debt. L5 is where it is paid. The concrete shape (pre-ruled, I ratify,
do not inflate):

- **Spider combat `wall` row: `+1/0 → +1/+1`.** The four L1-descended
  spider combat templates — `spider-soldier`, `spider-scout`,
  `spider-spinner`, `spider-elite` — and their promoted variants
  (`spider-veteran-soldier`, `spider-knight`, `spider-weaver`,
  `spider-stalker`) gain the banked `armor: 1` on the shared `wall`
  row. Attack stays `+1` (L3-ruled, L4-carried). This is the climbing
  defender finally _durable_, not just _biting harder_, on its identity
  planes — the exact thing the L3 arbitration §3 deferred here.

- **Full corner coverage.** This is the second half of the L3 §3.B
  ruling ("ramping to full corner coverage by L5"). At L3/L4 the
  reduced plane sets (4 planes at L3, 3 at L4) meant the wall gradient
  only bit on the planes those rooms used. L5 is the first scenario
  reopened to all 6 planes; "full corner coverage" means the spider's
  `wall` affinity is now live across _all four_ wall planes
  (north/south/east/west — the schema collapses them to one shared
  `wall` row, so this is realized by the 6-plane room _using_ all of
  them, not by a new data field) plus the spider's `spider-corner-cross`
  passive resolving corner adjacencies across the full plane set. The
  Bedroom's ceiling-premium route and bed-bisected floor mean the
  spider's off-floor identity is finally _spatially complete_.

This is _small in data_ (one sub-field, one shared row, on eight
templates) and I do **not** over-reach it — the same restraint I showed
declining `ant wall −1/−1` at L3 and `+3` at L4. I do not ask the
arbiter to inflate the armor point past `+1` or to dock the ant. The
ant combat `wall` row stays `−1/0`; ant floor/ceiling unchanged; queens
and ant support/casters and neutrals stay `wall 0/0`. The gap closes
_upward, on the spider side_, exactly as the L3 arbitration §4
specified — the curve's intended "enemy got more dangerous" closure,
not an ant nerf. What I insist on is that it _register_: the banked
payoff landing at its designed 6-plane home must be a _felt_ −2 to −3pp
spider swing, not tuned to ~0 so the concealment rebound runs the table
uncontested. A banked callback tuned to absent is the L3 "weak must not
be absent" failure inverted onto the spider's _promised_ payoff — the
worst version, because it was _owed_.

### Under-Bed concealment — a player tool the spider must be allowed to fight, not a hard counter to the spider's whole identity

The Under-Bed concealment POST is the ant's L5 tool and the §5
rebound's mechanism — I do not contest its placement or that L5
rebounds. I contest only its _framing as an uncontestable hard counter_
to the spec spider's information identity. The spec spider _is_ the
information faction: it scouts the ant's pheromone trail, it ambushes
what it saw coming (TBS §1.5). Under-Bed denies the trail for parties
_garrisoned on that one POST_. The ant advocate will frame this as "the
ant finally controls what the enemy knows." Fine — but the spider's
identity is not "the spider sees everything"; it is "the spider
_hunts_." Concealment that makes the ant _literally unscoutable and
therefore un-ambushable_ at L5 — four scenarios before hypnotize-full
and the L8/L10 climaxes — risks erasing the spider's _core verb_ at the
exact moment the curve only asks for a +5pp rebound, not a +15pp
collapse of the spider's defining mechanic.

My position on the concealment balance framing (Gameplay-owned, §4a):

- **The fog-immunity must be _positional and bounded_, not global.**
  The semantics are fixed (engine dep #7, frozen): a party _on_ the
  `concealment` POST emits no trail. Good — that is bounded by
  _occupation_. The spider's tactical answer must remain real: a party
  that _leaves_ Under-Bed to advance on the Dresser-Top objective
  _resumes emitting a trail_ and is scoutable/ambushable again. The
  concealment is a _staging_ advantage, not a _cloak the whole
  approach_ — the bed blocks the floor, Under-Bed is
  plane-transition-only (level-progression-plan §2 L5), so the ant
  _must_ surface onto the ceiling premium route to reach the objective,
  and _there_ the spider's now-complete `ceiling +1/+1` and
  full-coverage `wall` affinity is the hunt waiting for it. That is the
  spider's answer in the room — and it is _why_ the plane-affinity
  completion landing at L5 _with_ concealment is correct design, not a
  coincidence: the ant's info-denial tool and the spider's off-floor
  durability payoff debut _together_, the escalation and the answer in
  one room, exactly the §3.D / L4-§3.2 doctrine.

- **The win-rate weight is ~+4 to +5pp ant, not +6 to +8.** The §5
  rebound is ~60% → ~65%, a _+5pp_ recovery. If concealment alone is
  priced at +6 to +8pp it _over-shoots_ the rebound back toward ~68%,
  erasing the L4 spike's hard-won shape and flattening the whole
  L4→L5→L6 structure (L6 is the ~55% Stairs low). Concealment is the
  rebound's _engine_ but it is _budgeted against_ the plane-affinity
  completion's −2 to −3pp — the net is the controlled +5pp the curve
  prices, not an uncontested ant runaway. The same logic the L4
  arbitration used (the Light-Switch as the spike's _ceiling_, not its
  counterweight): here the plane-affinity completion is the rebound's
  _ceiling_, the lever that keeps the licensed +5pp from becoming an
  unlicensed +8pp.

### Hypnotize light debut — "light" must not be "absent"; this is my L3 through-line, owed symmetrically

Ruled §3.H: hypnotize debuts light at L5, full power L8. I do not
relitigate. My L3-and-since through-line applies exactly: **"weak" /
"light" must be a real, _present_ mechanic, not tuned to zero.** The ant
will ask for an aggressively short control-duration cap. A cap is
correct — the ruling §3.H itself made the duration a real learner-safety
knob and I conceded that structure (it is the _spider's_ own callback
doctrine: first contact light, designed climax at L8). But "light" must
still _seize the party and matter for a few turns_, or the L5 debut
fails its pedagogical purpose (the player must actually _feel_ losing a
neutral to learn to play around it before L8 weaponizes it at full
power against the queen, §4.3.3). The locked spec engine values are
`minControlTurns 5 / maxControlTurns 10`. A reasonable light cap is
`maxControlTurns` in the **3–4** band with `minControlTurns 2–3` — a
real seizure the player feels and must respond to, recoverable but not
cosmetic. I will _not_ accept a cap so tight (e.g. 1 turn) that the
hypnotize resolves before the player can perceive or respond to it —
that is "tuned to absent," the precise failure my through-line exists
to prevent, and it would leave L8's full-power climax a _cold open_
(the exact thing §3.H's light-debut structure was ruled to avoid).

### Why this serves the spider fantasy (interest)

L5 is the scenario where the spec's promise to the _defender_ — made at
L3, deferred through L4 — is finally kept: the climbing,
ceiling-and-wall faction becomes genuinely _durable_ on its own planes,
at the first room with the plane space to show it. The Bedroom's
geometry is built for this: the ceiling is the premium route, the bed
forces the ant up off the floor, and _up there_ the spider's now-complete
off-floor affinity is the hunt. The ant gets a real new tool
(concealment) — and a spider that can only watch the ant cloak its
staging, with no answer, is the boring-but-balanced failure inverted
onto the spider. The answer is the plane-affinity completion landing
_in the same room_: the ant denies the trail at Under-Bed, surfaces to
the ceiling to reach the objective, and meets a spider that is now hard
to kill exactly where the ant must go. Escalation (the ant's info tool)
_with an answer_ (the spider's banked durability at its designed home),
both debuting together — the §3.D / L4-§3.2 doctrine applied
symmetrically. That is the curve rebounding because the _player adapted
to a real tool against a real, dangerous defender_ — the interesting
reason, not "the spider's promised step was tuned away so the tool
runs free."

## 2. Natural-language rebuttal — answering the ant's L5 brief

The ant's brief is disciplined and concedes every placement and the
pre-ruled plane-affinity values. It contests two tunables: (a)
Under-Bed concealment must be priced as the _dominant_ L5 driver and
the rebound's mechanism; (b) hypnotize-light capped hard at
`maxControlTurns: 3`. I largely converge, with two corrections.

**First — I agree concealment is the rebound's engine; I contest
"dominant" if it means "uncontested."** The ant is right that
Under-Bed is the player-favorable mechanism of the §5 ~65% rebound and
must not be tuned to cosmetic — the "dead flavor" failure I myself named
at L3 cuts here too, symmetrically, and I concede it. Where I correct:
the ant's framing prices concealment at "+4 to +6pp" and the
plane-affinity completion as a small "−2 to −3pp." Run forward, +6
concealment − 2 affinity = ~+4pp = L5 ~64%, fine; but +6 − 2 with the
ant's _floor_ affinity reading drifts to ~67% — over the licensed ~65%.
The arithmetic only lands the ruled rebound if concealment is the
_engine_ and the plane-affinity completion is its _ceiling_ (the L4
Light-Switch logic, inverted): concealment ~+4 to +5pp, affinity
completion a _felt_ −2 to −3pp, net the controlled +5pp the curve
prices. "Dominant" yes; "uncontested" no — the spider's banked payoff
is the budgeted counter-pressure that keeps the rebound from
over-shooting, exactly as the ant itself argued the armor sub-field
must be "small precisely so the rebound stays a rebound." We agree on
the _structure_; I insist the affinity side of it actually _register_
at −2 to −3, not be quietly tuned to ~0.

**Second — on the hypnotize cap, we converge on a cap and nearly
converge on the number.** The ant asks `maxControlTurns: 3`. My
through-line demands "light ≠ absent." `maxControlTurns: 3` with
`minControlTurns ≤ 3` is _inside_ my acceptable 3–4 band — it is a real
seizure (3 turns is long enough to feel and respond to, the player
loses tempo, learns the mechanic) and recoverable (not the 5–10 of full
power). I _concede_ `maxControlTurns: 3` as acceptable _provided
`minControlTurns` is not driven to 1_ (a 1-turn floor would make the
expected control window collapse toward cosmetic). My ask:
`maxControlTurns: 3, minControlTurns: 2`. The ant and I have
_independently_ converged: both want a hard data-cap (not a gesture),
both put it at 3, both reject full-power-at-L5. The residual is one
integer — the `minControlTurns` floor (ant implies ≤3 and is silent on
the floor; I pin it at 2 so "light" is _present_). This is the L3/L4
convergence profile again: the adversarial exchange has collapsed the
whole L5 component set to two narrow tunable questions with both sides'
best case on record.

**Third — recruit-as-order: fully conceded, win-rate-neutral, no
contest.** Ruled §3.C (spider won the slot, ant won the goal). It is
the existing 25%/tier-2 recruit fielded as a charisma-gated order on
the `ant-mage`/`ant-archmage` line — army-building tempo fluency, an L8
runway, ~0pp. I concede it entirely; it is a ratification, not a
debate. Likewise the plane-affinity _values_ are pre-ruled and I have
ratified them, not relitigated — I contest only that the ruled −2 to
−3pp actually _registers_ and is not tuned to absent.

Net: I concede every placement (all ruled). I ratify the pre-ruled
plane-affinity ramp at its banked values (`spider combat wall +1/0 →
+1/+1`, full corner coverage, ants unchanged) — and insist its ruled
−2 to −3pp swing actually _register_ (not be tuned to ~0 so concealment
runs uncontested). I converge with the ant on a hard hypnotize
data-cap; residual is one integer (`minControlTurns 2` floor, my ask,
vs the ant's implied ≤3 with no floor). Concealment is the rebound's
engine; the plane-affinity completion is its _ceiling_ — net the
controlled, licensed +5pp ~65% rebound, not an uncontested ant runaway.
