## 1. Natural-language argument — opening

### The ant doctrine at L7, and why L7 is the rebound

My through-line is unchanged across six rooms: the ant experience is a
logistics-and-tempo puzzle, and the descent toward ~50% by L10 is that
fantasy maturing, not breaking. L6 was the resumed descent — the spec's
defender finally became the proactive hunter, the spiders came down the
terraces, and the curve dropped to its hardest-but-fair ~55%. **L7 is
where the curve breathes back up, and that is correct, not a balance
failure I should apologize for.** Roadmap §5 and mechanic-distribution
plan §4 both price L7 at ~64–65%: a clean rebound above L6's ~55% low,
the L6 arbitration itself stated L7 is "separated above ~64." The curve
is non-monotone here by explicit license (§5: "the curve doesn't need
to be monotonic; an occasional surprising spike … is a good thing").
L4→L5 was the licensed rebound; L6 was the binding monotone resumed
descent; **L7 is the next licensed non-monotone recovery**, and it must
read as _the player gaining favorable tools in an open arena_, not as a
stat re-tune and not as a monotone step.

This is the room the roadmap built for the ant to come into its own.
The Level PA's Living Room is the tier's largest, most _open_ arena —
three small furniture clusters in an otherwise open field
(level-progression-plan §2 L7). After the 1-D pipe, the island, the
no-flank corridor, the bisected bedroom, and the terraced gauntlet, L7
is the _first room where the ant can maneuver freely_. An open arena
favors the attacker with combined-arms tools — and L7 is where the ant
_gets_ those tools: commander cards debut, and the assembled combos
(Royal Onslaught, my answer-combo) finally turn on. The rebound is
_earned by the player gaining its toolkit at the room built to use it_.

### Commander cards are the single new high-cognitive mechanic — and they are mine to make legible

The mechanic-distribution plan §2 places commander cards at L7 as the
one bolded (§3.4.3) new high-cognitive mechanic of the room. Mechanics
memo §1.3 frames cards as a gold-sink — a Risk-2210-style deck the
player spends accumulated currency on for one-shot tactical effects.
The roadmap §6.5 ties the card economy to the world-loop shop; the
between-scenario shop schedule (roadmap §6.5) deliberately puts **no
shop after L7** — which means the card economy at L7 is fed _in-scenario_
by the Remote currency POST, not by a rest-stop purchase. That is the
whole reason the Level PA put a currency POST in this specific room
(level-progression-plan §2 L7 §6: "Remote feeds the card economy that
starts paying off here").

My ask on cards: they must be a real, data-expressible toolkit that the
ant AI _actually plays_, not dead deck-flavor. The §4d / L4-§9 lesson is
the spine of this project — a ruled mechanic the frozen AIs never
exercise measures ~0pp (plane-affinity) or +39pp (the L4 Light-Switch).
A commander-card deck the capture-chain ant AI never draws from is the
exact same trap: it changes nothing, L7 collapses to a generic
capture-post grind, and the rebound doesn't land. Cards must be
specified so the ant AI's policy _spends_ the Remote-fed currency on
deck plays during the assault — or they need a **binding within-loop
AI-doctrine constraint** that makes the ant AI play cards, on the
explicit L6-§3.1.2 / L4-§9.3(b) pattern, with a measurable ship-gate.

### The assembled combos are my rebound engine — turn Royal Onslaught ON for the ant

The L4 arbitration §3.J ruled the combo _components_ live at L4 but the
_assembled_ combos roster-gated OUT to L7. I conceded that cleanly at
L4 and I do not reopen it. L7 is where they turn on. The engine
mechanism is verified (`engine/battle-abilities.ts`,
`combo-abilities.test.ts`): a combo fires when a same-faction partner
party of the right composition is adjacent (Chebyshev ≤ 1, same plane)
to the shooter and both have the MP — Royal Onslaught needs an ant
**mage party adjacent to an ant worker party**; Venom Storm needs a
spider **spinner party adjacent to a spider queen/spinner party**.
"Turning the combo ON at L7" is therefore _purely a roster-composition
change_ — composing the L7 rosters so the right partner pairs are
deployable adjacent in the open arena. No ability-definition change, no
engine change. This is the cleanest possible §4b-compliant delta.

Royal Onslaught is _the ant's rebound engine_. The open Living Room is
the _only_ room so far where the ant can actually position a mage party
beside a worker party and keep them adjacent through an assault — the
pipe, corridor, and terraces all denied this; the open arena enables
it. My non-negotiable: the L7 ant roster must be composed so Royal
Onslaught is _assemblable and the ant AI assembles it_. That is the
mechanism of the licensed rebound — the player's combined-arms payoff
landing at the room built for maneuver — and like cards it must be
AI-exercised, not dead roster-flavor, or it measures ~0pp the way
plane-affinity does.

### Venom Storm comes online too — and that is the rebound's ceiling, not its negation

I do not pretend L7 is all upside for the ant. The same §3.J ruling
turns **Venom Storm** on at L7 for the spider (spinner+queen
composition), and the mechanic-distribution plan §2 also debuts
**spider blitz at 5%**. Those are the spider's counter-pressure in this
room. I accept them. They are the rebound's _ceiling_ — the budgeted
counter that keeps the licensed +9pp recovery from over-shooting into
an unlicensed second peak (the L5-arbitration §3.2 inverted-Light-Switch
logic, applied again). My position is exactly the symmetric one the
spider made at L5/L6: the _engine_ of the rebound is the player's new
toolkit (cards + Royal Onslaught + Remote economy), the _ceiling_ is
the spider's Venom Storm + blitz debut. Net the controlled ~64%,
separated above L6 ~55 and above the L8 ~50 continuation.

### The Remote currency POST — economy payload, and the engine reality

Level owns the Remote node; Gameplay owns the economy payload (the
per-turn value/direction). Here is the load-bearing engine fact I want
on the record before the spider over-reaches: **the engine is frozen
(§4b) and there is no native `goldPerTurn` / currency field on
`postSchema`.** Gold is a `world.ts` between-scenario world-loop concept
(`engine/schemas/world.ts:66-67`); the `score.ts` POST-owned bonus is
L1-bathroom-hardcoded (`SCORED_POST_IDS`), not generic; and there is
**no shop after L7** (roadmap §6.5). A "bonus gold per turn" payload
expressed as a brand-new POST field is an engine change and is
**forbidden**. The only per-turn, occupation-bounded economy lever the
frozen engine _actually honors_ is the shipped `postSchema.healingRate`
field (`engine/end-of-turn.ts:181-182`: each end-of-turn, every unit of
the party occupying a friendly POST regenerates `healingRate`) — which
is _exactly_ the mechanism the L6 arbitration §3.2 ruled the
POST-occupation economy through, for the identical §4d reason (it is a
first-order attrition-optimizer input the AIs read; a `wall` gradient
is not). My ask: the Remote economy payload is a **per-turn
`healingRate`-class occupation economy on the Remote POST, owned by /
favoring the ANT**, framed as the in-scenario "currency" that feeds the
ant's sustained-assault tempo (the data-expressible analogue of "cards
paying off here" — the ant out-attrites and out-tempos because it holds
the Remote). It is the rebound's economy engine, AI-exercised because
regen is a first-order input, §4d-robust by construction.

### No plane-affinity delta — and that is correct

Per §4d I do not ask for, and do not accept, any plane-affinity change
at L7. The L6 state (spider combat `wall {1,1}` + ceiling `{1,1}` +
full corner coverage) carries forward byte-identical. This is not me
conceding something — it is me refusing to let the L7 budget be wasted
on a lever §4d proved inert across L3/L4/L5/L6 (~0pp every time). L7's
~9pp rebound must be carried by levers the AIs _exercise_: the
commander-card plays, the assembled Royal Onslaught, the Remote
occupation economy, the Level-owned open geometry. Plane-affinity is a
latent identity layer; it is not, and must not be budgeted as, the
reason L7 rebounds.

### Why this serves the ant fantasy (interest)

A glass cannon's recovery room must be a recovery for a _legible_
reason. L7's reason is clean: after the hardest room of the first half,
the player breaks into open ground and _finally has its full toolkit_ —
cards to spend, the combined-arms combo to assemble, a currency node to
hold and out-attrite from. The descent didn't break; the player grew
into its kit at the room built for it. The player who learned
info-control at L5 and tempo/verticality at L6 now learns
_combined-arms maneuver_: in the open, you assemble your pieces and
spend your economy to overwhelm. That is the ant doctrine — out-think,
out-maneuver, out-combine, don't out-slug — taught by the first room
that lets the ant breathe. The rebound is _visible_ (the player can see
its cards, its combo firing, its Remote economy ticking) and _earned_
(by playing the kit well in the open), with the spider's Venom Storm +
blitz as the legible counter-pressure that keeps it honest.

## 2. Natural-language rebuttal — answering the spider's L7 brief

The spider will argue (consistent with its identity brief) that Venom
Storm coming online + the blitz debut are _its_ L7 payoff and must
register loudly as real counter-pressure, not be tuned to zero like
plane-affinity; that the Remote currency POST should not be a one-sided
ant subsidy; and that the rebound must be _ceilinged_ by spider tools so
it doesn't float L7 back toward L5's 66. I largely **converge**, with
three corrections.

**First — Venom Storm + blitz must register, and we agree on that;
where I correct is the same §4d point I made for my own tools.** The
spider and I agree the rebound must be _earned and ceilinged_, not a
free ant stat. Where I correct: the spider's instinct will be to price
Venom Storm as a fat unconditional spider buff. Run that against the
actual frozen AIs — the same lesson §4d / L4-§9 burned into this
project. Venom Storm only fires if the spider AI _composes and keeps a
spinner party adjacent to a queen/spinner party through a battle_
(`battle-abilities.ts` adjacency predicate, Chebyshev ≤ 1). If the L7
spider roster isn't composed for that, or the fortress AI doesn't
maintain the adjacency, Venom Storm measures ~0pp — the plane-affinity
fate, a third time. So the spider's _real_ ask, correctly stated, is
_the same as mine_: the L7 spider roster must be composed so Venom
Storm is assemblable **and** the spider AI assembles it — an
AI-exercised roster-gate-IN, with the same kind of binding within-loop
AI-doctrine constraint + measurable ship-gate the L6 arbitration
applied to the spider sortie. We converge on the _shape_; the residual
is pricing, which the curve resolves.

**Second — the Remote currency POST is ant-favoring, and that is the
rebound's engine, not an unfair subsidy.** The spider will say a
one-sided ant currency node erases its L7 counter-pressure. It does
not. The Remote economy is the _engine_ of a _licensed_ rebound — L7 is
priced at ~64% _by the roadmap_, the player is _supposed_ to be ahead
here; an ant-favoring economy node is the mechanism that delivers the
ruled recovery, exactly as Under-Bed concealment was the ant-favoring
engine of the L5 rebound (L5-§3.2) and the spider did not get to call
_that_ unfair. The spider's counter-pressure is delivered by Venom
Storm + blitz (its tools, online in this room) — _that_ is the
rebound's ceiling. A spider-favoring or neutral Remote would flatten
the licensed rebound the way a spider-favoring Light-Switch would have
collapsed the L4 spike (L4-§3.1) — the curve forbids it. The Remote
favors the ant by the same §3.4.4-curve logic that gave the ant the
Light-Switch direction and the Under-Bed engine: it is the only
direction that lands the licensed ~64%.

**Third — the rebound must not over-shoot, and I price my own engine
honestly to prevent that.** The spider is right that an over-tuned
ant rebound that floats L7 back toward L5's 66 erases the L6→L7→L8
shape (~55 → ~64 → ~50). I concede that entirely and I price my own
tools to prevent it: the Remote economy + cards + Royal Onslaught are
the _engine_ (the dominant +pp), and Venom Storm + blitz are the
_ceiling_ (the budgeted counter that holds the net at the controlled
~64, not ~68+). This is the L5-§3.2 / L6-§3.4 engine/ceiling structure
applied a fourth time, symmetrically: my engine, the spider's ceiling,
net the licensed rebound, separated above L6 ~55 and above the L8 ~50
continuation. I do not over-reach; I want the _ruled_ ~64, not a
runaway ant peak.

Net: I concede every placement (all ruled — commander cards at L7
§2/§3.4.3; assembled combos roster-gate-IN at L7 §3.J; Venom Storm +
blitz online at L7 §2; Remote currency POST the Level-owned node with
the Gameplay-owned economy payload §4a #3 / §5 case 3; **no
plane-affinity delta §4d**). I ratify the carried-forward L6 state
byte-identical. I contest exactly the _specification shape_ of the
levers the arbiter has not yet fixed in data: (a) commander cards and
Royal Onslaught must be AI-exercised (the ant AI provably draws/plays
cards and assembles the combo in a seed-robust majority), or carry a
binding within-loop AI-doctrine constraint + measurable ship-gate (the
L6-§3.1.2 / L4-§9.3(b) pattern), **not** dead deck/roster-flavor (the
§4d trap); (b) the Remote economy payload must be a per-turn
`healingRate`-class occupation economy **favoring the ant**, expressed
through the existing shipped field (engine frozen, no new field), the
_engine_ of the licensed rebound — with Venom Storm + blitz as the
budgeted _ceiling_, net the controlled ~64.
