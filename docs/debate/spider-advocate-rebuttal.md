## 1. Natural-language rebuttal

The ant opening is disciplined and largely honest. It concedes the L4/L6/L8
dips, accepts the pheromone-erasure floor, and gives up the score-tiebreaker
subsidy. That credibility is real and I will not squander mine by reflexively
contesting everything. I concede two of its three hardest claims outright,
hold the third with a concrete amendment, and then surface three NEW
contested points the ant opening exposed that neither opening priced in.

### 1.1 Royal Jelly application → L2: CONCEDED, and it strengthens the spider plan

The ant calls jelly-at-L2 its single most important placement. I agree, and
I concede it cleanly — not as a courtesy, but because it is _good for the
spider experience_, which is the test I apply.

The ant frames the pipe escort as a logistics puzzle. My opening (§1.3)
framed the same scenario as the birth of spider _ambush_ — retreat plus
pheromone-erasure converting spiders from turrets to ambushers at
pinch-points. These two framings are orthogonal, not rival. Jelly is an
_ant-internal_ resource-routing decision (push the dose forward vs. stage a
runner). It does not touch the spider toolkit, the spider's information
weapon, or the pinch-point ambush geometry. In fact it _helps_ the spider
fantasy: an escort that must also manage a forward jelly supply line is a
_richer_ target for a trail-erasing ambusher. The spider's L2 question is
"where do I blind them and spring," and a defender springs on a more
interesting victim when that victim is juggling logistics. The ant's own
"≈0 to +2pp" estimate is consistent with my §5 read of L2 at ~72%: jelly is
skill expression, not a power injection. Conceded, logged as
mutually-reinforcing, not as a spider cost.

One amendment I attach: the ant opening _under-claims_ erasure. It calls
erasure "minimized" in the pipe "because the escort's position is nearly
deducible anyway." That is true for the escort's _gross_ position but false
for the thing that decides the scenario — _which plane the escort is on and
when it switches_. In a 4×16 two-plane tube, plane-state is the entire
tactical variable, and erasure denying the ant a fresh read on the
escort's plane at the U-bend trap is exactly where the L2 dip to ~72% is
honestly earned. I am not contesting the floor (it is fixed); I am putting
on record that erasure's L2 weight is "decides the pinch-point," not
"cosmetic," so the arbiter does not under-tune the spider's only L2 teeth
on the ant's framing.

### 1.2 Asymmetric plane-switch + plane-affinity held to L4+: PARTIALLY CONCEDED — I split the bundle

This is the ant's strongest argument and it is correct _as stated about the
bundle_. The ant says introducing the §1.3/§1.4 cluster naked into the
stripped L1–L3 kit craters ants 10–18pp below the §5 floor (72%/70%) and —
the part that actually moves me — _teaches the player the ant doctrine is
unviable before they own the toolkit (jelly/recruit/combo) that makes
asymmetry a puzzle rather than a punishment_. That is a real interest
argument, not a homer win-rate plea, and §3.4.4 makes the §5 curve the
binding arbiter. I will not pretend a −15pp L3 is defensible. My opening
argued L4 for plane-switch already; I am not retreating from a position I
held.

But the ant bundled two mechanics of very different magnitude and asked for
one gate for both. I split them, because the split _protects the §5 curve
better than the bundle does_:

- **Asymmetric plane-switch (TBS §1.4, the 10–15pp lever): hold at L4, full
  strength.** L4 is the licensed ~60% spike. The spider's signature
  flanking maneuver _is_ the honest engine of that spike. The ant concedes
  the L4 dip and concedes combos land at L4 as the ant's answer. So at L4
  the player meets the spider flank AND the ant counter (Royal Onslaught)
  in the same scenario. That is the correct, balanced pairing and I accept
  the ant's L4 framing for this lever exactly.

- **Plane-affinity (TBS §1.3, the 5–10pp lever): debut at L3, deliberately
  weak.** Here I contest the bundle. Plane-affinity is a passive _stat
  gradient_ (spiders better off-floor, ants better on-floor), not an
  _order_ the player must learn to issue. It carries near-zero cognitive
  load — it is a terrain stat, in the same class the ant opening uses to
  justify Queen-proximity at L1 ("a stat rule, not an ability"). The ant
  cannot have it both ways: if a passive stat interaction is L1-legal for
  ants because it is a stat rule, a passive plane stat gradient is L3-legal
  for spiders on identical reasoning. And L3 _needs_ it: the Kitchen has a
  Counter-Edge height-advantage POST (§4.1) — a height/plane POST with no
  plane-affinity is a dead flavor. Concretely answering the §5 objection:
  the ant's own magnitude is "5–10pp" _at full tuning in open maps_. At L3,
  in a 10×10 with a center island and the spider kit still lacking
  venom/blitz/hypnotize, a _weak_ affinity gradient is a ~3–5pp swing, not 10. L3 target is ~70%; a 3–5pp spider lean lands L3 at ~65–67%, inside
  loose tolerance and _below_ the L4 ~60% spike, preserving monotone
  descent into the licensed L4 dip. The bundle-at-L4 _flattens_ L3→L4; the
  split _shapes_ it. The curve is the arbiter's standard — I am meeting it,
  not pleading around it.

So: plane-switch held at L4 (concede the ant's gate for the big lever),
plane-affinity pulled to L3-weak (contest the bundle, with a curve-explicit
defense). Net: I gave the ant its headline lever's gate and took the small
one early on its own stat-rule logic.

### 1.3 Venom-blast + blitz → L6+: venom-blast PARTIALLY HELD (L4-weak), blitz CONCEDED to L7

The ant's argument is that venom-blast is sustained-damage attrition that
punishes the low-HP glass cannon in exactly the protracted fights the ant
doctrine says to avoid, before ants have combos/cards/promotion to kill
fast. This is the ant's second-strongest point and it is _partly_ right —
but it mischaracterizes venom-blast to win the gate.

Venom-blast is a _burst_ spit (my opening §1.4: "single-target damage
tool," "spiders can now spit venom for burst damage"). It is the spider's
_reason to initiate_ — the fix for the round-28/29 "spiders too reactive"
weakness. The ant's frame ("sustained-damage tool that punishes the low-HP
ant in protracted fights") describes the _Venom Storm combo_ (L7, my §1.5),
not the L3/L4 component. A burst initiator does not create attrition fights;
it _shortens_ them — which is doctrinally _neutral_ for a faction whose
whole identity is "win fast or lose costly." A glass cannon is not
specially punished by burst; it is punished by _grind_. The ant has aimed
its strongest objection at the wrong mechanic.

That said, I priced the ant's real concern: a spider initiator before the
ant burst toolkit can over-tilt early scenarios. So I move off my opening's
L3 and meet partway:

- **Venom-blast: debut L4, low magnitude.** L4 is the licensed ~60% spike;
  a weak spider initiator is _within_ the intended dip, not a breach of a
  72%/70% floor (the ant's −8 to −12pp figure assumes an L2–L4 _full_-power
  debut; a deliberately weak L4 debut is ~3–4pp, absorbed by the spike the
  ant already endorsed). This costs the spider one scenario of initiative
  (L3 stays reactive) in exchange for landing inside the curve the arbiter
  binds to. Honest convergence.

- **Spider blitz (5%): conceded to L7.** My own opening already proposed
  L7, _not_ the L6+ the ant feared. We agree. I log it as conceded so the
  arbiter sees no daylight here. Blitz at L7 (player fluent, reads it as
  spice not "the tutorial lied") with peak impact at L10 still lets spiders
  escalate — the surprise _is_ the escalation; it does not need an earlier
  weaker debut, and an earlier one would waste the reveal. Held position
  unchanged, agreement logged.

### 1.4 NEW contest #1 — Retreat at L1 (the ant opening exposed this)

The ant opening claims retreat should be **L1, both sides**, and explicitly
predicts the Spider Sub-Agent will be "neutral-to-favorable" and that this
is "cheap to win." It is not cheap, and I am not neutral. I contest it.

Retreat is symmetric in _rule_ but asymmetric in _identity_. My opening
§1.3 argued retreat+threat-assessment at L2 is _the birth of spider ambush_
— the single mechanic that converts spiders from turrets to ambushers, the
spec's core spider identity, debuting in the pipe escort built for it.
Granting retreat at L1 spends that identity reveal in the stripped tutorial,
where the spider kit is hunker-at-web + one info ability and there is no
pinch-point geometry for an ambush to read against. The spider's ambush
debut becomes tutorial noise — exactly the waste I refused for hypnotize.
The ant's pedagogical case ("the low-HP unit that cannot retreat just
dies") is real _for ants_ but it is an argument for ant _survivability_ at
L1, not for spending the spider's signature behavior a scenario early. §3.3
says retreat "likely L2"; the user's verbatim is "fold in early," and L2 —
the second scenario, the first real test, shop-gated after — _is_ early.
"Early" does not mean "the stripped tutorial." Hold the line: retreat at L2.

### 1.5 NEW contest #2 — Recruit-as-order at L3 (the ant opening exposed this)

The ant wants recruit-as-order at **L3** (charisma-gated, with
bio-evolution) so the player is fluent before L8's recruit-or-die. The
anti-fun argument (meeting a win-condition mechanic cold at the spike) is
sound _in principle_ — it is the same callback logic I used for hypnotize.
But the ant smuggled it in under §3.4.3 by claiming "class change arrives"
is _one_ concept covering bio-evolution AND charisma-promotion AND
recruit-as-order. That is three mechanics wearing one coat. Bio-evolution
is locked at L3 (§3.4.5). Stacking charisma-promotion _and_ a new recruit
_order_ onto the same scenario violates the one-new-high-cognitive-mechanic
rule the ant elsewhere invokes against me. I do not oppose early recruit
familiarity; I oppose the L3 pile-up. Counter-placement: recruit-as-order
debuts **L5**, not L3 — after bio-evolution (L3) has its own scenario,
giving the player a clean two-scenario runway to L8, and L5 (Bedroom,
concealment POSTs) is a low-stakes capture map where a mis-timed recruit
costs nothing. This _grants the ant's interest claim_ (fluency before the
spike) while _honoring the cognitive-load floor_ the ant only enforces when
it suits the ant.

### 1.6 NEW contest #3 — Pre-game placement at L1: conceded with a symmetry condition

The ant wants pre-game placement on at L1 as the ant route-planning
identity. I concede the mechanic (it is the setup phase, not a stripped
ability) — but log a condition the ant opening omitted: placement must be
_symmetric in information_. If the ant places knowing fixed POST locations
(L1 is fixed-POST per §3.3) while the spider AI places without an equivalent
defensive-anchor heuristic, L1 drifts above the 80% spec ceiling and breaks
the _hard_ L1 floor/ceiling. Conceded, conditioned on the spider web-garrison
getting a placement heuristic of equal strength so L1 stays inside
`[65%,80%]`.
