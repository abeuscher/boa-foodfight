# Spider Faction Sub-Agent — Opening Position (Round 1)

**Debate:** Tier-1 mechanic-distribution plan (L1–L10), per roadmap §6.2.
**Advocate for:** the SPIDER experience, mechanical identity, and the
enemy-AI's job of providing escalating challenge.
**Scope:** which mechanics introduce at which scenario. No code; argument +
structured summary only.

---

## 1. Natural-language argument

### 1.1 The spider's job is to be a good enemy, and a good enemy escalates

The win-curve in roadmap §5 is the frame for everything I argue here. L1 is
~75% ant, L10 ~50%. That 25-point closure is _the spider's arc_. The ant
player gets a steadily richer toolbox; the spider must gain tools too, or
the curve closes for the wrong reason — ants getting better while spiders
stand still. A curve that closes because the _enemy got more dangerous_ is
the interesting version; a curve that closes because the _player
out-levels a static AI_ is the boring-but-balanced failure mode the user
explicitly named in §6.2. So my through-line is: spiders should _acquire
their identity-defining tools across the tier on a schedule that makes each
new room feel like the spiders learned something_, not front-load them
(which breaks the L1–L2 tutorial) and not back-load them all to L10 (which
makes L3–L8 a slog of the player beating an unarmed defender).

I will engage the win-curve honestly. I am not asking spiders to dominate.
I am asking that the _mechanism_ by which the curve closes be the spider
threat profile rising — because that is both the spec-faithful reading
(game-outline: spiders are the defending/ambushing faction; L1 enemy
behavior is tutorial-transparent and "later scenarios reduce explicit
pattern disclosure") and the interesting one.

### 1.2 L1 — the stripped tutorial must teach web-defense, and nothing else

Per §3.2, L1 is spider-queen + spider-soldier, abilities stripped to queen
ultimate, basic fight/defend/run, and one information ability. I fully
support this. The spider identity that _must_ be legible at L1 is the most
basic one: **spiders defend a web and ambush approaching parties.** The
roadmap (§4.1) sets L1's win condition as "capture spider-web (defended
POST template)" and game-outline makes spider behavior explicitly briefed
by Sergeant Antonio. The L1 spider kit should be: hunker at the web,
defensive posture (the spec's Run/Fight/Defend posture is enough to teach
the player that spiders _want_ to be attacked on their terms), one
information ability so the persistent-fog layer (the §3.1 hard floor — L1
is simple fog, _not_ pheromone-erasure) has something to interact with.

The thing I most want to flag at L1, on spider-as-good-enemy grounds:
**spider heal-priority web-defense (R18) should be ON at L1, but in its
mildest tuning.** It is not a "new mechanic" the player must parse — it is
the _reason the web is a hard target_, and the entire L1 win condition is
"take the web." If the web garrison doesn't heal-prioritize, the web is
just another POST and the tutorial fails to teach why spiders are a
defending faction. The Ant Sub-Agent will likely argue heal-priority makes
L1 too hard for a learning player. My answer: it is _legible_ hardness —
Antonio briefs it, the player can see the web garrison out-sustaining a
weak poke, and the lesson ("don't dribble parties at the web, mass and
commit") is exactly the lesson L1 should teach. Tune the heal magnitude
down, don't remove the mechanic. Removing it makes L1 a different game than
the one the tier builds toward.

What must NOT be at L1, and I'll defend the ant player here too because an
unreadable tutorial hurts both factions: **hypnotize, venom-blast, Venom
Storm combo, asymmetric plane-switch, day/night, pheromone-erasure.** Every
one of these, introduced at L1, makes the tutorial unreadable. Hypnotize
turning the player's own soldier against them in scenario one — before they
understand combat — is a feel-bad that teaches nothing. I concede this
freely; it is also good for spiders, because a spider faction whose
signature subversion tool debuts as tutorial noise is a wasted reveal.

### 1.3 L2 — retreat, pheromone-erasure, and the escort: the spider's first teeth

L2 is the pipe, an escort mission (§4.3.1), and per §3.1/§3.3 it is where
pheromone-erasure and retreat both land. This is the single most important
scenario for spider identity in the early tier, and I want to fight for it.

**Retreat + threat-assessment (R15/R16)** at L2 is explicitly user-called
(§3.3). For spiders this is the birth of _ambush_ as a readable behavior:
a spider party that threat-assesses and declines a bad fight, then re-engages
the escort at a pinch-point, is the first time the player sees spiders
_play smart_ rather than _sit_. The pipe geometry (4×16, one-dimensional,
plane-switch the only flank per §4.1) is purpose-built for this. I argue
retreat must be at L2 and not later — it is the mechanic that converts
spiders from "turrets" to "ambushers," which is the spec's core spider
identity.

**Pheromone-erasure** at L2 (a §3.1 hard floor — cannot be earlier anyway)
is the spider's first information weapon. game-outline: "Some enemy unit
types can erase pheromone trails." This is _spider_ fog warfare. In the
escort scenario it is genuinely threatening (a spider that erases its trail
near a pinch-point ambushes blind), and it is the honest mechanism for the
§5 dip to ~72% at L2.

I will _not_ ask for hypnotize at L2 even though §4.3.1 notes "Hypnotize is
particularly threatening here (a hypnotized escort party is
catastrophic)." That note describes hypnotize's _eventual_ role in the
escort archetype, not its debut scenario. Hypnotizing the escort in the
player's _second-ever_ scenario, right after they've met retreat, violates
§3.4.3 (at most one new high-cognitive mechanic per scenario — retreat +
pheromone-erasure is already the L2 budget; the roadmap treats erasure as
the headline and retreat as the user-mandated early fold-in). Asking for
hypnotize here would be a homer over-reach and I decline it. Hypnotize's
home is L8 (see §1.6 below) — debuting it earlier as a _light_ threat at
L3–L4 is the compromise I will defend.

### 1.4 L3–L5 — the spider gains a damage spike and a subversion tease

L3 is the bio-evolution debut (§3.4.5, locked) and class-change groundwork
(§3.1, L3+ floor). The spider's mid-tier acquisition should begin here.

**Venom-blast at L3.** This is the spider's first real offensive ability
beyond "fight posture." It is a single-target damage tool — low cognitive
load, one sentence to brief ("spiders can now spit venom for burst
damage"), satisfies §3.4.3. It gives the spider AI a _reason to initiate_
rather than only defend, which the round-28/29 history shows was the
recurring spider weakness. Placing it at L3 (not L1–L2, where it would make
the tutorial a damage-race the learning player loses; not L7+, where
spiders would have spent five scenarios as pure punching bags) is the
honest middle. The §5 curve has L3 at ~70% — a mild dip — and a single
spider damage tool is exactly a mild dip.

**Asymmetric plane-switch around L4.** The TBS memo (§1.4) makes spider
plane-switch a free corner-passive vs. ants paying an order. This is a
_huge_ spider-identity mechanic — it is the literal mechanical expression
of "spiders flank around the chain." I argue L4 because: (a) L4 is the
POST-randomization shock (§4.1; §5 has L4 dipping hard to ~60%, the first
"spike toward spider"), and a spider mobility tool _is_ the honest engine
of that spike; (b) the L4 hallway (6×16, multi-plane) is the first map
where flanking has room to matter; (c) earlier than L4 and the tutorial
player can't track why spiders teleport across corners. I expect the Ant
Sub-Agent to push this to L6+ on the grounds it's too strong early. My
rebuttal: §5 _wants_ a spike-toward-spider at L4 ("interesting > fair"
licenses it). Denying spiders their signature mobility until L6 flattens
the curve and removes the user's explicitly-desired non-monotonic shape.

**Hypnotize introduced (light) at L4–L5.** I propose hypnotize _debuts_
mid-tier — L4 or L5 — but tuned weak (short control duration, low neutral
density) so the player meets the _concept_ before L8 weaponizes it. L5
(bedroom, concealment POSTs, §4.1) is a natural home: an info-asymmetry
scenario where a hypnotized neutral emerging from concealment is thematic.
This is a concession, not a demand: I am not asking for full-strength
hypnotize early. I am asking that it not be a cold open at L8. A mechanic
the player has never seen, deciding a special-objective scenario, is a
feel-bad even when it's the spider's tool.

### 1.5 L6–L7 — the POST-occupation fix and the combo payoff

**POST-occupation bonus at L6.** This is the round-28/29 fix that "finally
made spiders proactive" — the single most important item in my brief.
Before it, spiders turtled and the curve only closed via ant
mis-execution. L6 is the stairs eradication scenario (§4.3.2): "kill every
spider party… encourages aggressive play — turtling does nothing… timeout
likely ant-loss." That is _exactly_ the scenario that needs proactive
spiders — a turtling spider AI in an eradication map is trivially farmed
and the scenario is dead. POST-occupation bonus at L6 is what makes spiders
_come down the stairs to contest landings_ instead of clustering on the top
one to be picked off. The §5 curve has L6 at ~55% ("geometry novelty favors
the defender"). I argue the honest reason it favors the defender is
_precisely_ this: a proactive spider that occupies stair-landing POSTs.
Without POST-occupation bonus, L6 is not a 55% scenario, it's a 75%
ant-stomp and the curve breaks.

**Venom Storm combo at L7.** The combo abilities (mechanics §1.2) extend
party-adjacency, and §4.1 notes "cards economy starts paying off here" —
L7 (living room, 14×14, first map larger than 10×10) is the first map with
room for two spider parties to stage a combo at a chokepoint. Venom Storm
(venom-blast + venom-blast combo) is the spider's late-tier spike-damage
moment, the thing that "breaks the late-game grind toward POST-hold
timeout" (mechanics §1.2 explicitly). It _requires_ venom-blast (L3) to
have been around long enough that the player understands the component
before the combo lands — L3 debut, L7 combo is a clean 4-scenario runway.
§5 has L7 rebounding to ~65% (player has cards); the combo is the spider
counter-pressure that keeps that rebound from being a runaway.

### 1.6 L8–L10 — hypnotize weaponized, blitz as the signature surprise

**Hypnotize weaponized at L8.** L8 is the cockroach recruit-or-die
scenario (§4.3.3): "Spider AI prioritizes hypnotize over offensive moves;
hypnotized cockroaches charge the ant queen." This is hypnotize's _designed
climax_ and the §5 curve's deliberate spike ("hard level before the end,"
§5 has L8 at ~50%). I argue full-strength hypnotize belongs at L8 — but
_only because_ the player has met it lightly at L4/L5 (my §1.4 concession).
A first-contact full-power hypnotize at L8 deciding a special objective is
a feel-bad; a _callback_ to a mechanic the player has fought before, now
turned up to eleven in a scenario built around it, is a payoff. This is the
strongest interest argument in my brief and I will defend the L4/L5 light
debut as the price of the L8 climax landing well.

**Blitz mode (5%) as a tier-wide signature surprise, debut ~L7, peak
L10.** The 5% blitz is the spider's "you thought you had this read"
moment. A 5%-probability surprise is _only_ interesting if the player has
enough scenarios left to be surprised again — debuting it at L10 wastes it
(one scenario, one roll). I argue blitz debuts ~L7 (once the player is
fluent enough that a low-probability disruption reads as _spice_, not as
_the tutorial lied_) and is most impactful at L10, the all-systems-on
finale where a blitz can genuinely swing a 50/50 game. Introducing it
earlier than L7 risks a learning player eating a 5% blitz they can't
contextualize and concluding the game is unfair.

**Day/night at L10** (§3.3 illustrative late slot). TBS §1.2 gives spiders
+1 attack/+1 agility at night. This is a strong spider tempo tool and L10
is its honest home — the finale, all systems on, genuinely close at ~50%.
Earlier and it's an un-briefable global state shift on top of an already
busy mid-tier. I'm not fighting hard for an earlier slot; L10 serves the
spider arc fine as the capstone tempo lever.

### 1.7 Honest engagement with the Ant Sub-Agent's likely counters

I anticipate four ant pushes and pre-concede where the homer move would
lose me credibility:

1. _"Heal-priority too hard at L1."_ — I hold L1 (it IS the tutorial
   lesson) but concede aggressive magnitude tuning. Mechanism stays;
   numbers flex.
2. _"Asymmetric plane-switch too strong at L4."_ — I hold L4 on §5-curve
   grounds (L4 is the licensed spider spike) but concede the corner-passive
   can ship range-limited (fewer corner pairs active early).
3. _"Hypnotize anywhere before L8 punishes learners."_ — Half-concede: I
   _want_ it before L8 but accept it must debut _weak_ (short duration,
   low neutral count) precisely so it doesn't punish learners; the L8
   version is the only full-power one.
4. _"Venom Storm + blitz stacked late is a spike wall."_ — Concede
   sequencing: blitz debut at L7 not L8, so L8's hypnotize spike and L10's
   blitz peak don't compound into one unreadable late-tier wall.

The places I will _not_ concede: POST-occupation bonus must be on by L6
(without it the eradication scenario and the whole back half of the curve
collapse into ant-stomps), and retreat/ambush must be L2 (it is the spec's
core spider identity and user-mandated early). Everything else is
negotiable on magnitude.

## 2. Structured summary (per contested placement)

### 2.1 Spider heal-priority web-defense — L1 (mild tuning)

- **Position:** ON at L1, magnitude-tuned down; do not defer the mechanic.
- **Faction impact:** It is the _reason_ the L1 win condition (take the
  web) teaches "spiders are a defending faction." Removing it guts spider
  identity at the exact moment the tutorial defines it.
- **Win-rate prediction:** Keeps L1 near the ~75% target with mild tuning;
  removing it would push L1 toward ~82%+ (ant-stomp, breaks spec floor).
- **Interest claim:** Legible hardness. Antonio briefs it; the player sees
  it; the lesson ("mass and commit at the web") is the right L1 lesson.

### 2.2 Retreat + threat-assessment — L2

- **Position:** L2, not later. User-mandated early (§3.3).
- **Faction impact:** Converts spiders from turrets to ambushers — the
  spec's core spider identity. Pipe escort (§4.3.1) is purpose-built for it.
- **Win-rate prediction:** Supports the §5 L2 dip to ~72%; absence leaves
  spiders passive and L2 nearer ~78%.
- **Interest claim:** First scenario where spiders visibly _play smart_
  (decline bad fights, re-engage at pinch-points). Ambush is the spider
  fantasy; this is its debut.

### 2.3 Venom-blast — L3

- **Position:** L3 debut (not L1–L2, not L7+).
- **Faction impact:** Spider's first offensive initiative tool; addresses
  the recurring "spiders too reactive" weakness without overwhelming the
  early tutorial.
- **Win-rate prediction:** Consistent with §5 L3 ~70% (mild dip); a single
  single-target damage tool is a small swing.
- **Interest claim:** Gives the spider AI a reason to initiate; makes
  spider turns watchable rather than purely defensive.

### 2.4 Asymmetric plane-switch — L4

- **Position:** L4 (concede range-limited corner-passive if pushed).
- **Faction impact:** The mechanical expression of spider flanking — the
  identity TBS §1.4 was written to deliver. L4 hallway is the first map
  with room for it.
- **Win-rate prediction:** Drives the §5 L4 spike-toward-spider (~60%);
  deferring to L6+ flattens the user's wanted non-monotonic curve.
- **Interest claim:** "Spiders flank around the chain" — harassment
  gameplay, not army-vs-army. The signature spider maneuver.

### 2.5 Hypnotize — light debut L4/L5, weaponized L8

- **Position:** Weak debut at L4 or L5 (short duration, low neutral
  density); full power only at L8.
- **Faction impact:** Spider's signature neutral-subversion tool; L8
  (§4.3.3) is its designed climax. The light debut is the cost of the L8
  payoff landing as a callback, not a cold open.
- **Win-rate prediction:** Light debut negligible (~1–2pp); L8 full power
  drives the deliberate §5 L8 spike to ~50%.
- **Interest claim:** A previously-met mechanic turned to eleven in a
  scenario built around it is a payoff; first-contact full power deciding a
  special objective is a feel-bad. Sequencing makes the spike land.

### 2.6 POST-occupation bonus — by L6 (NON-NEGOTIABLE)

- **Position:** Active no later than L6. This is the round-28/29
  proactivity fix.
- **Faction impact:** Without it spiders turtle; the L6 eradication
  scenario (§4.3.2) and the entire back-half curve collapse into
  ant-stomps. It is the mechanism that makes spiders a credible escalating
  threat at all.
- **Win-rate prediction:** Holds L6 at the §5 ~55% target; absence pushes
  L6 to ~75%+ and breaks the whole L6–L10 curve.
- **Interest claim:** Proactive spiders contesting stair-landings is the
  _reason_ the eradication scenario is a game and not a farm.

### 2.7 Venom Storm combo — L7

- **Position:** L7 (requires venom-blast since L3; 4-scenario runway).
- **Faction impact:** Spider's late-tier spike-damage moment; breaks the
  POST-hold-timeout grind (mechanics §1.2). L7 is first map large enough to
  stage two spider parties for the combo.
- **Win-rate prediction:** Counter-pressure against the §5 L7 ant rebound
  (~65%); keeps the rebound from running away.
- **Interest claim:** Two-party staged spike at a chokepoint — the spider
  "set the trap, spring it" fantasy at full expression.

### 2.8 Blitz mode (5%) — debut ~L7, peak L10

- **Position:** Debut ~L7, most impactful at L10.
- **Faction impact:** The spider's "you thought you had this read"
  signature surprise; needs scenario runway to recur, so not an L10-only
  debut.
- **Win-rate prediction:** Low average effect by design (5%); high
  variance contribution at L10 where it can swing a genuine 50/50.
- **Interest claim:** Rare disruption is spice only if the player is fluent
  enough to read it as spice; debut once they are, peak at the
  all-systems-on finale.

### 2.9 Day/night — L10

- **Position:** L10 capstone (not fighting for earlier).
- **Faction impact:** Strong spider night-tempo tool (TBS §1.2 +1 atk/+1
  agi); honest as the finale's capstone lever.
- **Win-rate prediction:** Supports the §5 L10 ~50% close as a tempo swing
  the player must plan around.
- **Interest claim:** Predictable strategic windows in the all-systems-on
  finale — a clean climactic lever, not mid-tier noise.
