## 1. Natural-language argument — opening

### The spider's L3 job

My round-1 through-line stands: the curve must close because the _enemy got
more dangerous_, not because the player out-leveled a statue (§6.2,
boring-but-balanced). My round-2 rebuttal §1.2 won the central L3 point —
plane-affinity is a passive stat gradient, "a stat rule, not an order," in
the same class the ant used to justify L1 Queen-proximity; consistency cut
both ways and the arbiter ratified L3-weak (mechanic-distribution plan
§3.B). I am not relitigating that. I am defending the _substance_ of the
debut so "weak" does not get tuned into "absent."

### The Kitchen is built for spiders, and the delta must register

The Level PA's L3 (level-progression-plan.md §2, L3): 10×10, **reduced
4-plane set — floor, ceiling, north-wall, east-wall**, a center island
splitting the room into two lanes, a Counter-Edge `+3 def` objective the
_defender reaches via interior lines_ — the Level PA's own words: "the
island gives the defender interior lines — slight spider nudge." The
spider is the defender here. This is the most spider-shaped non-special
scenario in the early tier and the mechanic delta must make that geometry
_legible_, or the Kitchen reads as a flat room with a rock in it.

Two of the Kitchen's four planes are **wall planes** (north-wall,
east-wall). In the L2 baseline (data/level-2/units.json) the `wall` row is
`0/0` for every template — _dead_. The spider climbs (movement: climbing);
the ant walks (movement: ground). The wall planes are _the spider's planes_
by movement-mode identity, and right now the data says fighting there is
neutral for everyone. That is the dead-flavor problem my rebuttal §1.2
named for the Counter-Edge POST: a height/plane POST with no
plane-affinity is dead flavor. At L3 the wall row must wake up, on the
spider's side, or the Kitchen's whole spider-favoring geometry is
mechanically inert.

### The spider position on the delta

1. **The L3 delta is the `wall` row waking up — agreed with the ant on the
   _locus_.** Floor/ceiling stay at the L2 baseline. One room of change.
   I concede this framing cleanly; it is correct and it is small. The
   contest is _magnitude_, not locus.

2. **Spider `wall +1/+1`, not `+1/0`.** The ant will demand attack-only.
   I contest. The spider identity on the wall planes is _the climbing
   ambusher who is hard to dislodge once it holds the high interior line_ —
   that is a _durability-plus-bite_ identity, not pure bite. The Counter-Edge
   objective is a `+3 def` POST the spider defends from the wall planes;
   a spider that hits harder there but dies just as fast does not teach
   "spiders defend the high ground" — it teaches "spiders poke from the
   high ground and fold," which inverts the spec's defending-faction
   identity at the exact scenario (a capture-post defense) where it should
   be clearest. The `armor +1` is one point. It is the difference between
   a readable defending faction and a flavorless one.

3. **Ant `wall −1/0` is correct and I do not over-reach it.** I will _not_
   ask for `ant wall −1/−1`. The ant's floor identity (`floor +1/+1`) is
   spec-faithful and the L3 delta should not double-tax the ant off-floor
   on armor — that _would_ be the multiplicative stack the ant fears, and
   conceding it here is honest, not generous: an over-taxed ant on the wall
   makes the Kitchen an unwinnable-feeling room, which fails the curve
   (target ~68%, not ~58%). Symmetry is not required by the schema and I
   do not claim it. Spiders get `wall +1/+1`; ants get `wall −1/0`. The
   asymmetry favors the spider _on the wall only_ and that is the point.

4. **Touch the combat templates the ant named — and I agree on queens
   untouched.** spider-soldier/scout/spinner/elite get `wall +1/+1`;
   ant-footman/archer/potato-bug/tank get `wall −1/0`. Queens untouched
   (the L1 web-defense lesson the arbiter ratified must not be muddied —
   the ant is right). Ant support/casters left at their existing weaker
   profile — agreed; I do not need to punish the ant's plane-switchers to
   win the Kitchen, the geometry does that.

### Why this serves the spider fantasy (interest)

The spec spider is the _defending, ambushing_ faction. The Kitchen is the
first non-special scenario whose geometry is explicitly built to reward
that (interior lines to a defended objective). If the wall delta is
attack-only, the spider that climbs to its identity plane and holds the
interior line _dies at the same rate as on the floor_ — the geometry
promises a defensive advantage the data refuses to deliver. `armor +1` on
the wall is the one number that makes the Kitchen's spider-favoring shape
_feel_ like a spider scenario rather than a neutral room the spider happens
to defend. That is the "the enemy got more dangerous" closure §6.2 wants —
not a stat the player can't perceive.

### The win-rate target

My rebuttal §1.2 committed to a number and I stand on it: at weak tuning
in the L3 10×10-with-island, with the spider kit still lacking
venom/blitz/hypnotize, a **3–5pp** spider lean lands L3 at the ~68% target,
below L2's measured 76% and above the L4 ~60% spike — preserving the
monotone descent the arbiter's §3.B ruling explicitly wants ("the split
_shapes_ the descent"). `wall +1/+1` on four spider combat templates across
the Kitchen's two wall planes, against `ant wall −1/0`, _is_ that 3–5pp.
Attack-only (`+1/0`) is the _bottom_ of that band or below it — it risks
L3 landing at ~71–72%, which does not clear "clearly below ~72% and near
68%." The arbiter's reconciliation (mechanic-distribution plan §4) requires
L3 _near 68%_; the armor point is what gets it there, not past it.

## 2. Natural-language rebuttal — answering the ant's L3 brief

The ant's brief is disciplined and concedes placement cleanly. It contests
exactly one thing: the `armor +1` sub-field, arguing attack-only is the
"legible pressure" version and `+1/+1` is an "unearned stat wall" / the
§6.2 boring-but-balanced failure mode. I rebut on three points.

**First — the ant inverts §6.2.** Boring-but-balanced is the failure where
the curve closes because the _player out-levels a static enemy_. An
attack-only wall debut is _more_ susceptible to that, not less: a spider
that hits 1 harder but dies at floor-rate on its identity plane is exactly
the static-feeling enemy the player out-levels with the bio-evolution and
promotion the ant gets _at this very scenario_ (L3, locked). The `armor +1`
is the thing that makes the spider _withstand_ the ant's new L3 army-builder
long enough to be a threat. Without it, L3's spider is a punching bag that
got a slightly sharper poke — that is the boring failure, wearing the ant's
own label.

**Second — the ant's "legible loss" argument actually supports `+1/+1`.**
The ant says the player must _see why_ a party died on a wall plane. A
spider that is _both_ harder to hit _and_ hits harder on the wall is the
_more_ legible lesson: "the wall is the spider's ground — do not fight them
there, contest the lane on the floor." That is a sharper, more teachable
boundary than "they poke 1 harder up there," which a glass cannon barely
notices in a burst-kill doctrine. The ant's own pedagogy goal is better
served by the readable terrain wall than by a sub-threshold attack tweak.

**Third — the arithmetic.** The ant claims attack-only _already_ produces
−3 to −5pp and the armor field pushes it to −5 to −7pp, citing my own
§1.2. Not so. My §1.2 number (3–5pp) was always _for the realized weak
debut in the island Kitchen_ — it was never quoted against an attack-only
sub-variant, because attack-only was not on the table in round 2. An
attack-only `+1/0` wall on a two-wall-plane map, against a defender already
favored by geometry, is a ~2–3pp swing — it _under-shoots_ 68% and lands
L3 at ~72%+, failing the arbiter's "clearly below ~72%" reconciliation
(mechanic-distribution plan §4). The `armor +1` is not what _breaks_ the
3–5pp band — it is what _delivers_ it. The ant is defending a number by
removing the knob that produces it.

I concede, genuinely: floor/ceiling untouched (agreed), ant `wall −1/0`
not `−1/−1` (I do not over-reach), queens untouched (agreed), combat
templates only (agreed), and the L5 ramp owns any further deepening
(agreed — `wall +1/+1` is the _L3_ endpoint, full corner coverage at L5 is
the ceiling-row and broader-template work, a separate ruled step). The
single residual contest is one integer: `armor` on the spider `wall` row,
`+1` (mine) vs `0` (ant's). Everything else has converged.

## 3. Structured summary

### Position

The L3 delta vs L2 is the **`wall` row waking up** (locus agreed with the
ant): spider combat templates **`wall +1/+1`**, ant combat templates
**`wall −1/0`**, floor/ceiling unchanged from L2, queens and ant
support/casters untouched, combat templates only. The single contested
integer is the spider `wall` **armor sub-field: `+1` (spider) vs `0`
(ant)**. Full corner coverage / ceiling-row deepening is a separate ruled
L5 step, conceded.

### Faction impact

`armor +1` on the wall makes the climbing, defending spider _withstand_
the ant's L3 bio-evolution/promotion army-builder on the spider's own
identity planes — the legible "spiders defend the high interior line"
identity the Kitchen's geometry promises and the spec requires. Attack-only
leaves the spider a sharper-poking punching bag the player out-levels: the
§6.2 failure mode wearing the ant's label.

### Win-rate prediction

Spider `wall +1/+1` vs ant `wall −1/0`, four combat templates each, across
the Kitchen's two wall planes against a geometrically defender-favored map:
**−3 to −5pp ant off the L2 measured 76% → ~68%**, below L2 and above the
L4 ~60% spike — the monotone descent §3.B explicitly wants. Attack-only
`+1/0` is a ~2–3pp swing → L3 ~71–72%, _failing_ the arbiter's "clearly
below ~72%, near 68%" reconciliation (mechanic-distribution plan §4). The
armor point _delivers_ the ruled 3–5pp; it does not exceed it.

### Interest claim

The Kitchen is the first non-special scenario built to reward the spec's
defending/ambushing spider (interior lines to a defended Counter-Edge).
`armor +1` on the wall is the one number that makes that geometry _feel_
like a spider scenario — the enemy genuinely more dangerous on its own
ground (§6.2's good closure), and a _sharper, more teachable_ terrain
boundary for the ant ("the wall is theirs — fight on the floor") than a
sub-threshold attack tweak.

### Convergence

Locus **conceded** (wall-row only, one room). Floor/ceiling untouched,
ant `wall −1/0` not over-reached, queens untouched, combat-templates-only,
L5 ramp owns deepening — **all conceded**. Single residual contest: the
spider `wall` armor sub-field, `+1` vs `0`.
